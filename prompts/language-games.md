# Language Games for 4th–8th Grade

---

## 1. The Yes/No Game
**Time:** 5–10 min  |  **Players:** 3+

One person is "it." Everyone else asks yes/no questions — but "it" cannot answer with "yes" or "no." They must rephrase:

- "Absolutely."
- "Not at all."
- "That's correct."
- "Incorrect."

Catch them saying "yes" or "no" — they're out. Last one standing wins.

**Hard mode (older kids):** Ban additional words: *maybe, because, but, probably.*

---

## 2. Two Truths and a Lie
**Time:** 10–15 min  |  **Players:** 3+

Each person says three statements about themselves — two true, one false. Everyone votes on which is the lie. The liar reveals and explains.

**Vocabulary twist:** "I went to the \_\_\_\_ last weekend" — fill the blank with a word everyone must define before guessing.

**Good for:** Getting to know each other, building listening, spotting patterns in language.

---

## 3. Fortunately / Unfortunately
**Time:** 10–20 min (can loop)  |  **Players:** 4+

One person starts a story: *"Fortunately, we found a map to a hidden island."*

Next person: *"Unfortunately, the map was in a language nobody could read."*

Next: *"Fortunately, I found a translator app."*

Each turn alternates between fortunate and unfortunate. The story goes anywhere.

**Vocabulary twist:** Start with a list of 5–10 words. Each sentence must use one word from the list.

---

## 4. The Dictionary Game (Fictionary)
**Time:** 15–20 min  |  **Players:** 4+

One person picks an obscure word from a dictionary (or has one ready). Everyone else writes a fake definition. The reader reads all definitions (including the real one) aloud. Everyone votes on which is real.

**Scoring:** +1 point for fooling someone with your fake definition. +1 point for guessing the real one.

**For your group:** Use words from whatever you're studying — Latin roots, science terms, historical vocabulary. Pre-select 5–10 words before class.

---

## 5. Word Chain Category
**Time:** 10–15 min  |  **Players:** 3+

Pick a category:

- "Things you'd find in a medieval castle"
- "Verbs that describe how water moves"
- "Words with Greek roots"
- "Adjectives for describing a person's character"

Go around the circle. Each person says one word in the category. No repeats. If someone hesitates too long or repeats, they're out or the chain resets.

**Hard mode (older kids):** Each word must start with the last letter of the previous word.

---

**One rule for all games:** No phones. No writing it down. Oral only.
