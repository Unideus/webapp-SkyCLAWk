# SkyCLAWk Subscription Plan

## Goal
Keep the core astrology experience free, make the app feel generous, and charge only for persistent depth, saved work, and premium outputs.

## Pricing

- `Free`: $0
- `Plus`: $4.99/month max
- `Cosmic History Report`: one-time purchase, keep the existing `$19` position

## What stays free

- Generational timeline
- Skyclock
- Basic wheel viewing
- `Import to Natal`
- Standalone zodiac wheel popout
- Basic Auspicious scoring
- Free preview report

## What goes in Plus

- Unlimited saved profiles
- Cross-device sync
- Saved natal data and histories beyond the single free profile
- Advanced wheel overlays
- Lots and cycle tools
- Shadow tools
- Aspect search
- Export and print-friendly output
- Reminders and premium alerts

## UI rule

Do not remove paid features from the interface.

- Show advanced modals and controls in a disabled or grayed-out state
- Add a short lock message and upgrade CTA
- Let users preview what they would unlock

This is the right balance for this app because the wheel already has deep, premium-feeling controls, while the popout wheel remains free and should stay a top-of-funnel feature.

## Product rationale

The market pattern is consistent:

- `Astro.com` keeps discovery free and sells deeper chart/report access
- `Sanctuary` and `Nebula` monetize recurring guidance and account features
- `Co-Star` stays lightweight and social-first, with less emphasis on heavy gating

SkyCLAWk should land between those models:

- generous free discovery
- low-cost Plus for power users
- separate one-time report sales for high-intent buyers

## Recommended entitlement split

### Free

- First chart render
- Basic timeline browsing
- Basic natal import
- Popout wheel
- Basic reports

### Plus

- Persistent profiles
- Saved settings
- Advanced modals
- Extra overlays
- Search, compare, and export
- Premium alerts

### One-time report

- Full natal / cosmic history PDF
- Deeper interpretation
- Exact conjunctions and timing narrative

## Notes

- Keep `Plus` cheap enough that it feels like an upgrade for enthusiasts, not a hard paywall.
- Keep the popout wheel free so the app still has a strong, shareable demo mode.
- The premium boundary should be depth and persistence, not basic navigation.
